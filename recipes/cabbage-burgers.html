Cabbage Burgers

Home | Jump to Recipe

Description

These meat-filled pocket pastries became a staple of the American Midwest when they were brought here by the Volga Germans. They are also known as "Kraut Burgers", "Bierocks", "Fleischkuekle", or "Runzas" (readers from Nebraska might recognize that last one). They're also economical, filling, and delicious!

Ingredients

  • 1 head cabbage, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 lbs ground beef
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 2 (16-oz) boxes hot roll mix (or favorite bread dough)
  • 1 tbsp shortening to grease pan

Recipe

  1. Mix cabbage, onion, ground beef, and salt and pepper together in a large bowl.
  2. Transfer mixture to a large steam pot and steam until cabbage and onion are tender. Drain.
  3. While cabbage mixture is steaming, mix boxed hot roll mix according to directions on box.
  4. Roll dough on floured board until approximately 3/8 inch thick. Cut into 6 inch squares.
  5. Place a tablespoon of cabbage mixture into the center of each square.
  6. Fold corners of each square into the center so that all corners touch. Pinch the corners together and pinch the edge of each opening well until filling is fully sealed inside dough.
  7. Turn upside down & place on greased pan. Allow to rise 10-15 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350°F.
  9. Bake in oven until browned.
