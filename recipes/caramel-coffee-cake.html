Caramel Coffee Cake

Home | Jump to Recipe

Description

A challenging but rewarding family staple - the caramel has claimed many a pan in the kitchen... (My grandma's the only one that can get it right reliably). Apparently the rest of the internet calls these "crumb bars". We've always known it as coffee cake - but not your typical coffee cake!

Ingredients

Roll Dough:

  • 1 pkg. dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup lukewarm water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup shortening
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup milk, scalded
  • 1 egg, slightly beaten
  • 3 1/2 cups flour

Caramel Topping:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups hot water
  • 1/3 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3/4 cup sweet cream

Rivvel Topping:

  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups flour

Recipe

  1. Soften yeast in lukewarm water.
  2. In a large bowl, combine sugar, shortening, and salt; stir in hot milk and cool to lukewarm.
  3. Add softened yeast, egg, and 2 cups flour. Beat well.
  4. Gradually add remaining flour or enough to make a soft dough.
  5. Cover and allow to rise in a warm place until double (about 1 1/2 hours).

While dough is rising, make the caramel and the rivvel topping:

  1. Caramelize the sugar and brown sugar in a heavy skillet, stirring continuously so that it does not burn.
  2. Stir in 1 cup very hot water and stir until sugars are dissolved.
  3. Stir in flour and remaining 1/2 cup hot water. Stir until mixture has thickened.
  4. Add butter, vanilla, and cream. Stir again. Set aside.
  5. For the rivvel, mix butter, sugar, and flour with hands until consistency of large crumbs.

Finally, return to the dough, assemble everything, and bake:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Once dough is double size, punch it down. Turn out on a lightly floured surface and roll into an oblong shape about (or less than) 1 inch thick.
  3. Place in a greased jelly roll pan (or baking sheet) and pat out dough to the edges of the pan.
  4. Spread caramel on top of roll dough. (Note: if using a shallow baking sheet, caramel may drip off edge of pan while baking, causing a difficult mess of burned sugar to clean out of the oven; leave space along the edges when spreading caramel and make sure dough is slightly thicker around the edges to avoid this).
  5. Sprinkle rivvel crumbs over top of caramel topping until evenly distributed. Can use as much or as little as desired (leftover rivvel makes an addicting, snackable sweet treat; keep refrigerated).
  6. Bake in 350° oven for 30 minutes. Cut into squares and serve.
